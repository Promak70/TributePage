Tribute to Marie Curie

Marie Curie was a pioneering physicist and chemist who conducted groundbreaking research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, the only woman to win in two fields (Physics and Chemistry), and the first person to receive two Nobel Prizes. Her discoveries of radium and polonium have paved the way for advances in cancer treatment and medical diagnostics.

Timeline of Major Events

  • 1867: Born in Warsaw, Poland.
  • 1891: Moved to Paris to study physics and mathematics at the Sorbonne.
  • 1898: Discovered the elements polonium and radium with her husband, Pierre Curie.
  • 1903: Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics

    Marie Curie shared this Nobel Prize with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel for their joint research on radiation phenomena, recognizing their groundbreaking work that expanded the understanding of radioactivity.

  • 1911: Awarded the Nobel Prize in Chemistry

    Marie Curie received her second Nobel Prize for her discovery of the elements radium and polonium, her work in isolating radium, and her studies on the nature and compounds of this remarkable element. She became the first person to win Nobel Prizes in two different fields.

  • 1934: Passed away due to aplastic anemia, a condition believed to be caused by prolonged exposure to radiation.
